Mission

Our focus is to advance the use of open source software and related technologies. We are the organizers and leaders of DC Python, a 501(c)(3) Python user's group; the creator and project lead for Pillow; the lead organizers and hosts of Plone Conference 2008; and the providers of successful open source software development, consulting, training, hosting and support for companies and organizations from Amtrak to USAID, in the U.S. and around the world, since 2004. Our past, current, and future endeavors allow us to continue to reinforce and expand our commitment, outreach, as well as our involvement with open source technology and the community that depends on it.

Pillow

Python Imaging Library

In 2010, Alex Clark created Pillow, a software development fork of the venerable Python Imaging Library. As of early 2017, Pillow has been downloaded over 15 million times, can be found in many major Linux distributions such as Ubuntu and Fedora Core, and is used by Fortune-100-serving companies like Atlassian. As of 2019, professional Pillow support is available from Tidelift.
